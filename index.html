Francesco Bertolotti


Contacts:

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Università Carlo Cattaneo - LIUC, where I teach the Dynamical System Design course and study how to apply and extend computational simulation methodologies to different kinds of complex social systems. I hold a PhD from LIUC – Università Cattaneo, with a thesis titled “Risk Preferences Adaptation in Complex Adaptation Systems.” The fields I am mostly interested in are:

  • agent-based modeling
  • risk preferences adaptation
  • generative artificial intelligence
  • complexity in healthcare
  • computational cliodynamics
I have been a visiting researcher at the University of Cambridge (Centre for the Study of Existential Risk), Eindhoven University of Technology (IS & IE group), and the University of the Balearic Islands (IFISC). I am also a research fellow at HD-LAB - Healthcare Datascience Lab, where I am involved in the use of computational methods to study and support the Italian healthcare system. I am a co-author of the book “L’intelligenza artificiale di Dostojievski".
