About the workshop

While artificial intelligence researchers primarily focus on the design, optimization, and capabilities of individual machines, complexity scientists possess the theoretical tradition and the methodological tools required to understand the intricate interactions between multiple AI systems, as well as the emergent behaviors within them.

The Intelligence, Complexity and Technology Lab was founded precisely with this vision in mind. We are actively tackling these themes within our lab, and our goal is to create a fertile ground for sharing ideas and addressing a perspective that is currently missing in the broader scientific discourse. Ultimately, this workshop creates a meeting point for researchers working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and complexity science, so they can connect and present ongoing work in this area.

We believe that bridging these two disciplines represents a major scientific challenge with profound implications across several dimensions. For AI research, the field can be greatly enriched by adopting diverse methodologies from complexity science, including both formal analysis and advanced simulation techniques. Simultaneously, for complexity science, artificial intelligence ecosystems offer a new, highly fascinating, and rapidly changing domain of study to test and expand theories of complex systems. Finally, for practitioners, as AI systems become more integrated into society, understanding how they interact with each other and their environment is of enormous practical relevance for building safe, robust, and scalable real-world technologies.
